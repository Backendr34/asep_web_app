Aggressive braking

Heavy braking without enough time to cool down can lead to brake overheating.

×

Poor quality parts

Low-quality or worn brake pads and rotors can cause brakes to overheat.

× Lack of maintenance

A lack of proper vehicle maintenance can contribute to brake overheating.

×

Damaged brake lines:

Road debris, corrosion, and vibrations can cause brake lines to develop cracks and tears.

×

Worn brake fluid reservoir

A worn out or damaged reservoir, or a cap that isn't properly sealed, can cause brake fluid leaks.

×

Leaking brake calipers

Loose, damaged, or corroded brake calipers can leak brake fluid.

×

Uneven braking

Your car may pull to one side when you brake

×

Excessive vibration

The surface of the discs may not be smooth, or they may be cracked or warped.

×

Loss of braking efficiency

Your car may take longer to stop or you may need to apply more force to the brake pedal.
